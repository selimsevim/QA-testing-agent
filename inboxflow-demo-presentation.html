What gets missed

The expensive failures are invisible until they ship.

Wrong branch

A non-clicker receives a click-only follow-up.

This means the actual customer journey does not match the intended automation logic.

Broken revenue path

The primary CTA lands on the wrong page or a dead destination.

The email can look fine while the conversion path is unusable.

Brand and compliance risk

Raw tokens, missing tracking, or broken unsubscribe flows go live.

These are small QA misses that become very visible to customers.

An autonomous launch gate for lifecycle campaigns.

Why semantic checks matter

Some QA cannot be solved by status codes.

A page can return successfully and still be the wrong page. A subject can be valid text and still mislead the recipient. That is where semantic reasoning helps.

Deterministic checks

Did the expected email arrive?
Did a persona receive the wrong branch?
Are UTM parameters present?
Are raw personalization tokens visible?

Gemini reasoning

Does the CTA destination match the campaign promise?
Does the unsubscribe destination actually look like opt-out?
Do subject, body, CTA, and goal align?
